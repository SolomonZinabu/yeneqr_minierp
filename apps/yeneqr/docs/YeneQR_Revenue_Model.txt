3. TRANSACTION FEES
================================================================================

Transaction fees are YeneQR's highest-margin, most scalable revenue stream.
They grow automatically as restaurants process more orders — no sales effort
required.

FEE STRUCTURE:

  Payment Method     | Starter    | Professional | Enterprise
  ──────────────────|────────────|──────────────|───────────
  Cash              | Free       | Free         | Free
  Telebirr          | N/A        | 1.5%         | Negotiated
  Chapa             | N/A        | 1.5%         | Negotiated
  CBE Birr          | N/A        | 1.5%         | Negotiated
  ──────────────────|────────────|──────────────|───────────

  Enterprise negotiated rate: 0.75%–1.25% based on volume.

HOW IT WORKS:

  1. Customer pays 500 ETB via Telebirr
  2. Payment provider takes their merchant fee (~1.5-2%)
  3. YeneQR takes 1.5% (7.50 ETB)
  4. Restaurant receives settlement: 500 - provider fee - YeneQR fee

  Settlement is handled automatically. Restaurant sees a clear
  breakdown in their payment dashboard: gross, provider fee, YeneQR
  fee, net settlement.

WHY THIS IS POWERFUL:

  - Zero marginal cost — we don't handle the money, we route it
  - Scales with customer success — as restaurants grow, we earn more
  - Predictable — based on order volume, which trends upward
  - Invisible — deducted from settlement, no separate invoice

4. PAYMENT PROVIDER PARTNERSHIPS
================================================================================

Beyond our own 1.5% fee, we earn additional revenue from payment providers
through referral and volume-based programs.

TELEBIRR (Ethio Telecom):
  - 40M+ users, dominant mobile payment in Ethiopia
  - Merchant referral commission: 0.3-0.5% of transaction volume
    we route to Telebirr
  - Volume bonuses at threshold milestones

CHAPA:
  - Leading payment gateway, growing rapidly
  - Revenue share: 0.2-0.4% of transaction volume
  - Co-marketing opportunities for mutual customer acquisition

CBE BIRR (Commercial Bank of Ethiopia):
  - Largest bank in Ethiopia by customer base
  - Merchant onboarding commission: one-time fee per new merchant
  - Transaction-based commission: 0.2-0.3%

COMBINED PROVIDER INCOME:
  On top of our 1.5% transaction fee, we earn an additional
  0.5-1.0% from provider partnerships. This effectively doubles
  our per-transaction margin.

  Example: 500 ETB transaction
  - YeneQR fee:        1.5%  =  7.50 ETB
  - Provider commission: ~0.7% =  3.50 ETB
  - Total per transaction:        11.00 ETB
  - Effective margin:   2.2%

5. ENTERPRISE & CUSTOM SERVICES
================================================================================

Enterprise clients represent high-value, high-margin revenue beyond
standard subscriptions.

SETUP & ONBOARDING FEE:
  One-time fee per enterprise client for:
  - Custom configuration and branding
  - Data migration from existing systems
  - Staff training (on-site, 1-2 days)
  - QR code generation and physical placement consulting
  - Floor plan design assistance

  Pricing: 15,000–50,000 ETB per client
  (Based on number of branches and complexity)

CUSTOM FEATURE DEVELOPMENT:
  Enterprise clients who want features built specifically for them:
  - Integration with their existing POS system
  - Custom reporting for their accounting team
  - Specialized kitchen station configurations
  - Custom payment settlement workflows

  Pricing: 5,000–25,000 ETB per feature
  (We already have the platform — custom work is incremental)

PREMIUM AI TRAINING:
  Train AI agents on the restaurant's specific menu, customer base,
  and business rules:
  - Custom menu knowledge base
  - Restaurant-specific upsell rules
  - Branded AI personality and greetings

  Pricing: 10,000 ETB one-time + 2,000 ETB/month ongoing

6. FUTURE REVENUE STREAMS
================================================================================

These unlock as YeneQR reaches scale (100+ restaurants, 6+ months):

SUPPLIER MARKETPLACE (Month 12+):
  Restaurants buy ingredients, packaging, cleaning supplies, and
  equipment regularly. YeneQR can become the procurement platform:
  - "Order Supplies" button in the dashboard
  - Connect restaurants with vetted suppliers
  - Commission: 3-5% on each order
  - Value: Restaurant orders from one place, supplier gets new customers,
    YeneQR takes a cut of a transaction that was already happening

  Potential: Average restaurant spends 50,000-200,000 ETB/month on
  supplies. 3% commission = 1,500-6,000 ETB/month per restaurant.

FEATURED LISTINGS & ADVERTISING (Month 9+):
  - "Popular Near You" placement in customer-facing shared menu pages
  - "New on YeneQR" featured spot
  - Push notification promotions to customers
  - Sponsored recommendations in AI Menu Concierge

  Pricing: 2,000-10,000 ETB/month per restaurant for premium placement

SMS NOTIFICATION FEES (Month 3+):
  - SMS costs: ~0.30-0.50 ETB per message (Ethio Telecom bulk rate)
  - Charge restaurant: 1.00 ETB per SMS
  - Margin: 0.50-0.70 ETB per SMS
  - A busy restaurant might send 200-500 SMS/month = 100-350 ETB margin

ANONYMIZED DATA INSIGHTS (Month 18+):
  At scale, aggregate data becomes valuable:
  - Dining trends by area, cuisine, time
  - Consumer spending patterns
  - Market intelligence for food industry

  Buyers: Food suppliers, market researchers, investors, government
  Pricing: Subscription-based access, 10,000-50,000 ETB/month

WHITE-LABEL LICENSING (Month 24+):
  License the YeneQR platform to operators in other African markets:
  - Kenya, Tanzania, Nigeria, Ghana, South Africa
  - Flat licensing fee + revenue share
  - We built it once, sell it many times

  Pricing: 50,000 ETB/month + 10% revenue share per market

KEY INSIGHTS
================================================================================

1. Transaction fees will OVERtake subscription revenue by Year 2-3.
   This is the SaaS flywheel: more restaurants → more transactions →
   more revenue → invest in product → more restaurants.

2. Provider partnerships effectively DOUBLE per-transaction margin.
   Always negotiate volume-based commissions when onboarding
   payment providers.

3. The free Starter plan is a growth hack, not a revenue source.
   Its purpose is to get restaurants onto the platform. Conversion
   to Professional happens when they want KDS, AI, or digital payments.

4. Enterprise is the highest-value segment per client but
   Professional is the volume play. Don't neglect either.

5. The supplier marketplace could become the largest revenue stream
   long-term. A restaurant spending 100,000 ETB/month on supplies
   with 3% commission = 3,000 ETB/month — that's 30% of the
   Professional subscription from an entirely different budget.

6. White-label licensing is exponential. One deal in Kenya could
   match the entire Ethiopian revenue with zero marginal cost.
